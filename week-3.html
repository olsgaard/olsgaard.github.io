Q6

θ, the proportion of samples that fail a battery of tests.

20 tests on randomly selected samples.

Assuming each test is independent, she assigns a binomial likelihood where X counts the samples which fail. Historically, new processes pass about half of the time, so she assigns a

Beta(2,2) prior for θ (prior mean 0.5 and prior sample size 4).

The outcome of the tests is 6 fails and 14 passes.

y <- 6*1 + 14*0 # (6 fails and 14 passes)
n <- 20

prior_alpha = 2
prior_beta = 2

alpha <- prior_alpha + y    #updated alpha
beta <- prior_beta + n - y #updated beta

alpha

## [1] 16
